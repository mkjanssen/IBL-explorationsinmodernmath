Nonetheless, we can still explore the ways in which changes in \(r_0\) affect the shape of the curves; our qualitative observations will still apply to real-world situations like the current coronavirus pandemic.

    Recall that \(r_0 \approx \frac{bN}{a}\) and assume our population size \(N = 10000\) and recovery rate \(a = 0.1\) are constant. Compute the three values of \(r_0\) that result from infection rates of \(b = 0.00005, 0.0001\text{,}\) and \(0.0002\text{.}\) In turn, plug these values into the applet and comment on the shape of the (green) infection curve: how tall is the spike of infected individuals, and at what time \(t\) is it at its highest point?
