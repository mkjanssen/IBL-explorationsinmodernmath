As it turns out, divisor methods (like Jefferson's, Adams', and Webster's) are not susceptible to these paradoxes. However, as we saw, they can violate quota. This is in contrast to Hamilton's method, which does not violate quota but is susceptible to these paradoxes. A reasonable person might wonder if there isn't some better method that both satisfies quota and cannot fall victim to these paradoxes. Fascinatingly, the answer is a resounding no.
