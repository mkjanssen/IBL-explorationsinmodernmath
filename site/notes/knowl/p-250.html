Most states have adopted a winner-take-all rule. That is, the winner of the popular vote in that state wins all the electoral votes the states have. The exceptions are Maine and Nebraska. In those states, the winner of the statewide vote gets the two electoral votes representing the state's senate delegation, while the remaining electoral votes go to the winners of the House districts. Each state has split its electoral vote twice: Nebraska in 2008 and 2020, and Maine in 2016 and 2020.
