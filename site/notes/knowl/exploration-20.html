Exploration 7.1.3.8. Hilbert's Grand Hotel.

Hilbert's Grand Hotel is a full-service luxury hotel, having as many rooms as there are natural numbers. The room numbers are \(1, 2, 3, 4, 5, \ldots\text{.}\) Suppose that every room is completely filled.

  1. A weary traveler arrives late in the night looking for a place to stay. Coud the manager find a way to give the traveler her own private room without having to ask anyone to leave the hotel?
  2. What if two travelers arrive? Three?
  3. What if infinitely many travelers arrive?
  4. What if Hilbert's Grand Hotel is empty and an infinite number of busses, each with an infinite number of passengers, arrived. Can room be found for all of them?
