Another idea closely linked to that of the electoral college and apportionment is redistricting: once a state has its apportionment for House seats following a census, it needs to draw district lines on its map to determine which geographic regions its House members will represent. This is known as a district map or district plan. However, the history of drawing lines on a map is fraught. This week, we'll take a look at some of the ways in which mathematics can help us describe or evaluate fairness.
