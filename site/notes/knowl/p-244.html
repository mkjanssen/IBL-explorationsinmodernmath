Given whole numbers \(m,n\text{,}\) the geometric mean is the number \(\sqrt{m\cdot n}\text{.}\) In our example, \(\sqrt{11\cdot 12} = 11.4891\text{;}\) this, then, is the number to which we'll compare our standard quotas in Hill's method. And thus, a standard quota of 11.494 would be rounded up to 12 rather than down to 11.
