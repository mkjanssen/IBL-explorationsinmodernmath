What we've just observed is an example of gerrymandering, the process of drawing district lines to give an advantage to a group of voters. Gerrymandering is named for Elbridge Gerry, the governor of Massachusetts in 1812, who helped draw state senate election districts to benefit his party. The Boston Weekly Messenger printed an editorial cartoon that drew the offending district as a dragon, dubbed the “Gerry-Mander” (see Figure 9.2.2).
