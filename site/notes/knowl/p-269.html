  • Contiguity: all parts of the district should be geographically connected.
  • Compactness: residents in districts should live relatively close to one another (though measuring this is hard, as we'll see).
  • Preserving political boundaries: districts should strive not to cut through cities, counties, or other municipal entities.
  • Preserving communities of interest: when possible, districts should not separate groups of voters who live near one another and have common political, social, or economic interests.
