Section 3.1 January 10, 2020: Getting To Know You (and the Cube)

Today we start with introductions to the course. Dr. Janssen introduced himself and gave an overview of the course (see Part I for more!), and then we worked on Exploration 3.1.1.
Exploration 3.1.1.

First, introduce yourself to the members of your group. Then, consider the questions:

  1. What are the essential qualities of play? That is, what makes one activity play, and another not?
  2. What does it mean to be playful in your own major disciplines?
  3. What does it mean to be an explorer in your major disciplines?
  4. What are you excited about this semester? Your answer can be from one of your classes or outside of your classes.
Write your answers on your whiteboards. We'll discuss your responses before working on Investigation 3.2.1.

Solution
We only really had time to discuss the first two questions. There were some common themes.
  1. Play: is enjoyable; is fair; will depend on the person; involves actively engaging with body/brain; is voluntary; can be strategic; is a leisure activity; is hands-on.
  2. In our major disciplines, playfulness: involves hands-on activities; creativity with lesson plans; uses manipulatives; allows us to play with/try on ideas (thought experiments); is social (involves relationship building); explore mathematical/physicals ideas; is a mindset; must engage the whole person; involves puzzles; etc.
  3. We didn't have time to talk about our answers to this question.
  4. Things we're excited about: getting into more of our major courses, continuing to learn, making new friends/growing together with old friends, and others.
One answer to question 1 that didn't come up is that an activity is often called play when there are no great stakes in the outcome. We can try to solve the Cube, play chess with our friends, or even play a basketball game, and in the end, it's just play. If it's competitive, it doesn't really matter whether we win or lose, and if it's a puzzle we're playing with of our own volition, the stakes are probably even lower.
Investigation 3.1.2.
What questions do you have about this course? Brainstorm a list on a piece of paper to be submitted to Dr. Janssen at the end of the class meeting.
Conclusion.

Today we got a sense for what this course will be like. We met the people in our initial groups and thought about play. Next time, we'll start studying our Cubes in more detail.
