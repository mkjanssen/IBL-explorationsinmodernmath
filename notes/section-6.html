Section 3.6 January 27, 2020: Magic Cube Moves III

We'll now examine one last Cube move and its effects.

Cube Move 3.6.1.
Consider the following sequence of cube moves:
\begin{equation*} \text{Edge Flip} = (M_R U)^3 U (M_R^{-1} U)^3 U \end{equation*}
Written less compactly, this move is: \(M_R U M_R U M_R U U M_R^{-1} U M_R^{-1} U M_R^{-1} U U\text{.}\)
Exploration 3.6.1.

Consider Cube Move 3.6.1.

  1. Which cubies are affected by this move?
  2. By performing this move several times, identify on the diagram below the effects the move has on the Cube.
  3. Using the cubie notation described in Convention 3.4.1, describe what is happening to the Cube.
  4. Given your answer to the previous question, exercise your agency as image-bearers and suggest a two- or three-letter name/abbreviation for this move.
  5. What is the order of the move?
Solving the Cube.

When you can consistently perform Cube Move 3.4.2, Cube Move 3.5.1, Cube Move 3.5.3, and Cube Move 3.6.1, you can use them to solve the Cube. You can use any strategy you want, but here is one to consider.

  1. Put the corners in the correct location.
  2. Put the edges in the correct location.
  3. Reorient any corners that need reorienting.
  4. Reorient any edges that need reorienting.
  5. Celebrate!
  6. Scramble the Cube and do it again.

Once you can consistently solve the Cube, make an appointment with Dr. Janssen to take your Rubik's Cube MFA. We'll spend all of next class working on solving our Cubes.

Conclusion.

Today we explored the last move we need to solve the Cube. We also described a strategy for solving the Cube 1 . Our method of solving the Cube is based on the corners-first (CF) method, which is distinct from the layer-by-layer (LL) method which is often the first solving method people learn. The first solution to the Cube, by Erno Rubik himself, was corners-first, and the first world speed-cubing record (22.95 seconds) was done corners-first.

Note that when you complete your Rubik's Cube MFA, you can use whatever strategy you want, but you will be asked to demonstrate competence on two of the four moves we explored (randomly chosen at the time of the MFA).

There are advantages and disadvantages to all methods. Advantages to corners-first are:

  • The move sequences are generally shorter in CF
  • There are just a few important move sequences to memorize
  • You generally don't "break" your existing work and so can recover from mistakes more easily
  • The solution can scale from a leisurely solve of a few minutes or more to quite fast solves
