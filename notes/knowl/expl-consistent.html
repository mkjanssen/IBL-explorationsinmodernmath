  1. Suppose Raymond begins with the case that the native is a knave. What can he conclude from the native's statement?
  2. Based on your answer to the previous question and the fact that Raymond thinks he is incapable of making mistakes, what does he conclude the native to be? Explain.
  3. Explain why your answer to the previous question gives rise to a contradiction.
  4. What does your answer to Questions 2-3 mean about Raymond's assumption that the native is a knave? Explain.
  5. Based on your reasoning thus far, what does Raymond now believe the native to be?
  6. Based on the native's statement, "You will never believe I am a knight," what is the true status of the native? Explain.
  7. Based on your answers to this exploration, explain why a reasoner who believes they are accurate and who meets a native who says, "You will never believe that I am a knight" will eventually become inaccurate.
