Question 2.0.9. How will each item of work be assessed?

Unlike "typical" college courses, there are no points or partial credit to be earned in this course. Instead, each item of work will come with its own specifications for completing the item at one of four potential levels, each of which is given an approximate interpretation below:

  • Exceeds expectations: this work is submitted on time, is extremely well done, clearly communicated, and could function as the official class solution.
  • Meets expectations: this work is generally correct, but either contains some minor mathematical errors which do not detract from the overall solution, or contains some unclear/incomplete communication.
  • Revision needed: the work contains some significant errors and/or the guidelines for preparing and presenting the solution were not followed, but you seem to be on the right track toward a passing grade.
  • Not assessable: the work was not turned in on time, is difficult to read, or contains significant enough flaws that you're probably better starting over.

Generally, E's and M's are considered passing, while R's and N's are not. Some assessments will be graded on a 2-level rubric (such as group presentations), some on a 3-level (such as the Rubik's cube quiz), and others on a 4-level (such as the other MFA's).
