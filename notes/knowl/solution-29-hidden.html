We had an in-depth discussion on this, and it all hinges on what it means for the implication \(P\Rightarrow Q\) to be true. Since a knave can only say false statements, the knave can only say things of the form \(P \Rightarrow Q\) when \(P\) is true but \(Q\) is false. Since the statement \(P = \text{"I am a knight"}\) is false, the knave cannot say anything of this form, regardless of the truth or falsity of \(Q\text{.}\)
