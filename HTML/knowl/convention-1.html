Convention 3.3.5.

The standard algebraic notation for moving the faces of the Cube is as follows: \(F, R, L, U, D\text{,}\) and \(B\) refer to clockwise quarter-turns (\(90^\circ\) rotations) of the front, right, left, up, down, and back faces, respectively, as you look at the face in question.

On the other hand, a \(-1\) superscript indicates a counterclockwise turn as you look at the face in question.

Finally, we can string moves together by writing them left to right, such as \(R F U L D\text{.}\) If we want to repeat a move, we use exponents in the usual way, such as \(R F^2 U (LD)^3\text{.}\)
