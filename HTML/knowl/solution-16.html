This moves cycles three edge cubies clockwise on the up face:

\begin{equation*} uf \to ul \to ur \to uf. \end{equation*}

Since it's a cycle of three cubies, it has order 3. Following our name for Cube MoveÂ 3.4.2, we suggested the name Three Edge Tango. We also observed that if you reverse the direction of the upper rotations, you move the same three cubies, but in counterclockwise fashion. That is, the move

\begin{equation*} M_R^2 U M_R^{-1} U^2 M_R U M_R^2 \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} uf \to ur \to ul \to uf. \end{equation*}
