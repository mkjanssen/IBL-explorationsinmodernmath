Solution 4.4.8.1.
  1. If Raymond believes the native is a knight, he will believe what the native tells him.
  2. Let \(P\) be the statement "I (the native) am a knight". Then Raymond believes \(P\text{,}\) which means he believes the native's statement, which is that he does not believe \(P\text{.}\) This is a contradiction, as Raymond is regular.
  3. He now believes the native is a knave.
  4. Raymond believes the native's statement is false.
  5. Since Raymond believes the native's statement is false, he does not believe that he will never believe the native is a knight. Thus, he does not believe that he believes that the native is a knave.
  6. No. In either case he begins by believing the native is one thing, and eventually believes that he does not believe it.
